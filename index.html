Efficient Multiplier Architectures

A real-time simulation of Radix-4 Booth Encoding & 4:2 Compressor Logic inside modern CNN Image Processors.

⚠️ Problem Statement

Modern Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) require massive amounts of Multiply-Accumulate (MAC) operations, bottlenecking performance and draining power in edge devices. Traditional standard multipliers are too slow, generate too many partial products, and consume excessive power for real-time AI processing.

🎯 Project Objective

To design, simulate, and verify a highly efficient hardware multiplier using Radix-4 Booth Encoding combined with a 4:2 compressor architecture. This design aims to reduce partial product generation by 50% and minimize critical path delay, providing a highly optimized processing element for next-generation CNN accelerators.

Architecture Benefits & Efficiency

Why use this specific processing element (PE) design? In heavy AI workloads, hardware efficiency is just as important as software accuracy.

1. Radix-4 Booth Encoding

By grouping bits during the multiply phase, Radix-4 reduces the number of generated partial products by exactly 50% compared to standard multipliers. This drastically lowers dynamic switching power consumption.

2. 4:2 Compressor Tree

Instead of chaining traditional 3:2 Full Adders, 4:2 compressors condense four inputs into two outputs in parallel. This yields a much shorter critical path delay, enabling higher clock frequencies.

3. Dedicated CNN Optimization

This pipeline is specifically tuned for MAC (Multiply-Accumulate) operations. Since Convolutional Neural Networks spend over 90% of their compute time multiplying matrices, optimizing this single circuit provides massive chip-wide performance gains.
